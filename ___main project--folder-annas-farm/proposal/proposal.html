Design Vision
A Colorado Geological Narrative

This project transforms two neglected common-space entry areas at Anna's Farm into distinctive community landmarks. The design tells a complete geological story drawn from the Colorado landscape: Flatirons mountain formations rising as a dramatic backdrop, canyon cliff walls carved by flowing water, and a dry creek bed winding through it all.

Three Geological Elements
Hero Element — Mountain Backdrop
Flatirons Formation

Colorado red flagstone slabs installed vertically at varied heights and tilts, composed to create the iconic mountain ridge silhouette of the Boulder Flatirons. This is the dominant sculptural element — the mountain range rising behind the landscape.

Secondary Vertical — Canyon Walls
Strip Stone Cliff Walls

Colorado red strip stone, 1–2 feet above grade, arranged in semi-arced formations along the bends of the dry creek bed. Where the creek curves, canyon walls rise — just as water carves through rock in Colorado’s natural canyons.

Ground Narrative — The River
Dry Creek Bed

River rock and granite stones shaped as a flowing creek, winding through each site. The creek passes through canyon walls at its bends and flows around the base of the Flatirons formation. Contrasting decorative stone fills the surrounding ground.

Site Context
Anna's Farm — Lafayette, Colorado

Both sites are located within the Anna's Farm community at approximately 5,350 ft elevation on the Colorado Front Range. Site 1 anchors the corner of Anna Thomas Parkway and Baseline Road. Site 2 sits ~400 ft north along Homestead Street. The Flatirons are visible to the west — the direct inspiration for the vertical stone formations in this design.

Proposed Installation

The larger site receives the full geological narrative. The Flatirons formation — multiple vertical red flagstone slabs — creates a dramatic mountain backdrop. A dry creek bed of river rock winds through the site, and where the creek bends, canyon cliff walls of Colorado red strip stone rise 1–2 ft, giving each curve geological drama. Six tons of native Colorado granite boulders anchor the composition. Ten solar accent lights uplight the vertical stone faces at dusk.
